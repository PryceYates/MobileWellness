Monthly challenge

Steps

Every team member logs their daily steps for the month. Whichever team has the highest daily average wins.

How it works

  1. Track your daily step count however you already do, phone, watch, or fitness tracker all work fine.
  2. Log your number under your team name at the end of each day, or in a weekly batch if that's easier.
  3. Each team's score is the average daily step count per person across the month, not a raw total. That's what keeps a small team competitive with a bigger one.
  4. Whichever team is running the highest daily average when the month closes out wins.

Why steps specifically

Step count is one of the simplest, most universally trackable movement metrics there is, and it's also directly tied to how your body handles glucose day to day. This one is a live version of The Momentum Chart framework: the daily act of logging your number is most of what makes the habit stick.

Research on glycemic variability in healthy, nondiabetic adults tracked both continuous glucose readings and daily step counts over two weeks, finding that daily movement patterns are closely tied to how stable blood glucose stays throughout the day.
