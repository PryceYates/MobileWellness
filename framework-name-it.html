Framework 02

Say It and Become It

A short daily practice for describing yourself as someone who moves, before it's fully true. Actions follow identity.

The problem it solves

Most people don't lack information about moving more. What's usually missing is a self-image that matches the behavior: it's hard to keep doing something that doesn't feel like "you" yet. This framework works on that gap directly, instead of waiting for enough reps to change how you see yourself on their own.

How it works

  1. Pick one identity statement that's slightly ahead of where you actually are: "I'm someone who moves during the workday," not "I'm an athlete."
  2. Say or write it once a day, ideally right before or after you complete a small movement action, so the identity and the behavior get linked.
  3. When you do the behavior, narrate it to yourself in identity terms: "that's the kind of thing I do now," not "I guess I did it today."
  4. Give it a few weeks. The statement should start to feel less aspirational and more descriptive.

Why it works

Research on habit and identity has found a strong link between how strongly someone identifies with a behavior and how consistently they repeat it, and the relationship runs in both directions, meaning repeated action can build the identity just as identity can drive the action.
