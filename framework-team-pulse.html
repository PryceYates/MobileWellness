Framework 05

The Team Pulse

A short weekly check-in ritual with coworkers. Movement sticks better when it's connected with friends, not just personal willpower.

The problem it solves

Solo movement plans quietly compete with everything else you're solely responsible for, and usually lose. Bringing coworkers in changes the equation: showing up isn't just about you anymore, and that shifts a habit from optional to social.

How it works

  1. Pick two or three coworkers and agree on a 60-second daily check-in: a shared Slack channel or a standing thread works fine.
  2. Each person posts one line: whether they moved today, and one word about how it felt. No explanations required, no judgment either way.
  3. Keep it low-stakes. This isn't a leaderboard, it's closer to a shared logbook that makes showing up slightly harder to skip.
  4. Rotate who starts the thread each week, so it doesn't quietly become one person's job to keep alive.

Why it works

Workplace-focused research on motivation consistently finds that relatedness, a genuine sense of connection to others working toward the same goal, is one of the more reliable drivers of sustained engagement, often outlasting reward-based or purely individual approaches.
