01 — Frameworks

Ways of thinking, not just to-do lists

Each framework targets a different piece of why movement doesn't happen on its own during a workday — friction, identity, or cueing. Start with whichever one describes your actual problem.

01

The Two-Minute Reset

Attach sixty to ninety seconds of movement to something you already do every day — every call that ends, stand up before opening the next tab. The habit rides on a cue you don't have to remember.

Read the framework →

02

Name It to Become It

A short daily practice for describing yourself as someone who moves, before it's fully true. Identity tends to follow repetition — this gives the language a head start.

Read the framework →

03

The Friction Audit

A five-minute worksheet for finding what's actually stopping you — shoes in the wrong place, a water bottle across the room, no clear first step. Remove the friction before adding more willpower.

Read the framework →

02 — Challenges

Built for teams, not leaderboards

Run these with coworkers. Each one is scored as a shared total, not a individual ranking — the goal is momentum you build together, not a winner and everyone else.

Live now

30-Day Standing Streak

Every team member logs one stretch of thirty-plus minutes standing or moving, once a day, for a month. The team total climbs together — no individual leaderboard.

Walking Meeting Week
