Frameworks

Ways of thinking, not just to-do lists

Each framework targets a different piece of why movement doesn't happen on its own during a workday: friction, identity, cueing, defaults, or the people around you. Start with whichever one describes your actual problem.

01

Elite Habits

Attach some physical movement to something you already do every day. The habit rides on a cue you don't have to remember.

Read the framework →

02

Say It and Become It

A short daily practice for describing yourself as someone who moves, before it's fully true. Actions follow identity.

Read the framework →

03

The Friction Audit

A five-minute activity for finding what's actually stopping you. Identify the friction that is killing momentum, so you need less "motivation".

Read the framework →

04

Default Settings

Redesign the defaults around you. This includes calendar blocks, meeting invites, or a standing desk. Make movement the easy option, not the exception.

Read the framework →

05

The Team Pulse

A short weekly check-in ritual with coworkers. Movement sticks better when it's connected with friends, not just personal willpower.

Read the framework →

06

The Momentum Chart

A simple visible tracker for making consistency obvious. What gets seen gets repeated. This makes it clear and reaffirming.
