Weekly challenge

Lunch Walk

Finish every lunch with a couple of laps around the building. This fills the movement gap most afternoons fall into, and helps keep energy more stable through the rest of the day.

How it works

  1. Every day this week, right after lunch, take a couple of laps around the building, or around the block if that's not available.
  2. Five to ten minutes is enough. This isn't a workout, it's a reset for the afternoon.
  3. Log it under your team once it's done.
  4. Each team's score is the average number of lunch walks per person this week. Highest team average wins for the week.

Why a lunch walk specifically

The timing isn't arbitrary. What you eat at lunch affects your blood glucose, and blood glucose swings are closely tied to the energy dip and foggy focus a lot of people write off as a normal part of the afternoon. A short walk right after eating is one of the simplest, best-supported ways to blunt that swing.

Research on glycemic load and mood in healthy adults found that people on a lower-glycemic diet reported significantly more energy and less fatigue than on a higher-glycemic diet, even when the two diets had identical calories. Read the full breakdown in the overview, or go straight to the study.
