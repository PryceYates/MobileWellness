Weekly challenge

Walking Meeting Week

One week. Swap one meeting a day — the kind that doesn't need a screen — for a walk. Small enough to actually finish, structured enough to notice.

How it works

  1. Look at Monday's calendar and flag one meeting each day this week that doesn't require slides, screen-sharing, or note-taking on a laptop.
  2. Take that meeting as a walk — outside if you can, hallway laps if you can't. Phone call or voice-only video works fine.
  3. Post a one-line recap in the team channel after: which meeting, how it went. That's the whole report.
  4. By Friday, count how many walking meetings the team collectively pulled off — five max per person, but the team number is what matters.

Why a weekly challenge

A week is long enough to build a small pattern but short enough to stay realistic — you get five chances to practice the swap without it becoming a permanent commitment you have to maintain. If it works well, nothing stops you from just... keeping it going.
