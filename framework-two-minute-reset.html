Framework 01

Elite Habits

Attach some physical movement to something you already do every day, so the habit rides on a cue you don't have to remember.

The problem it solves

Most movement plans fail for a boring reason: they depend on remembering to do them. A habit that needs to be recalled, decided on, and then acted on, every single time, is a habit that competes with everything else on your plate. It usually loses.

How it works

  1. Pick one thing you already do without fail during the workday: a call ending, a Slack notification, closing your laptop for lunch.
  2. Decide on a movement you can do in under two minutes: standing up, a lap around the room, a set of stretches.
  3. Do the movement immediately after the cue, every time, for two weeks straight, even on days it feels pointless.
  4. Don't stack more than one new cue at a time. One clean link is stronger than three shaky ones.

Why it works

Habit researchers describe this as tying a new behavior to a stable, high-frequency cue already built into your day: the more consistently the cue occurs and the more immediately the behavior follows it, the faster the pairing becomes automatic.
