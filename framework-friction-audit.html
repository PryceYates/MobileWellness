Framework 03

The Friction Audit

A five-minute activity for finding what's actually stopping you. Identify the friction that is killing momentum, so you need less "motivation".

The problem it solves

Willpower is a finite resource, and most sedentary days quietly drain it before 10am. Often the real obstacle isn't motivation, it's small, fixable friction: shoes across the room, no clear place to stretch, a calendar with zero white space. Fix the friction and the willpower problem often shrinks on its own.

How it works

  1. List the last three times you meant to move but didn't. Be specific about what happened right before you gave up on it.
  2. For each one, name the exact physical or logistical obstacle: not "I was busy," but "my walking shoes are at home" or "I had back-to-back calls."
  3. Pick the single easiest obstacle to remove this week. Move the shoes. Block ten minutes on the calendar. Put a sticky note on the monitor.
  4. Re-check next week. Removing friction is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time fix.

Why it works

Behavior-change research consistently identifies environmental cues and the reduction of friction as key factors in whether a repeated action becomes automatic: easier context, more consistent repetition. See the full picture in this systematic review of habit-formation research.
